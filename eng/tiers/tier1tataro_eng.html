By itself, it does not interpret or interpret anything, but simply shows the geographical distribution of various haplogroups (that is, common dna markers that are transmitted by male Y chromosome from father to son continuously up to the most distant common ancestor).
So, according to these studies, among our compatriots (in this case we are talking about the Central part of Russia, where the main Old Russian cities were located), speakers of Slavic, Baltic, Celto-Germanic languages are represented, Scandinavian, Caucasian, Semitic and some other haplogroups (in descending order).
